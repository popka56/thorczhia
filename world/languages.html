Languages

There are many languages in the world of Thorczhia and many different people and races that speak them. Here follows a list of all known languagues with descriptions about who speak them.

Precursor

Typical speakers: Linguists & historians.

The predecessor to Common. It was spoken by the ancient people of Oqpa a long time ago. Its writing can be found on many of their ruins and on many ancient machinations, even those used today. Many who grew up on Oqpa know some words or phrases of the languague.

Common

Typical speakers: All the Human Races.

The most spoken languague in Thorczhia. It is spoken by almost everyone, even Non-human races may sometimes know it.

Undercommon

Typical speakers: Drows.

While mainly spoken by Drows, many Non-human races choose to speak it over their own in certain situations. Especially with Humans, as Common and Undercommon share many similarities. Knowing one of the two allows you to easily understand the other if it is spoken to you.

Nak

Typical speakers: Humans of Na'ak.

The people of Na'ak were long disconnected from the rest of the world, because of the long war, and developed their own languague instead of speaking Common. Almost none on Na'ak can speak Common, but it is becoming more and more common now that the war is over. However, Nak is still seen as the primary languague.

Dwarvish

Typical speakers: Dwarves.

The new dwarven languague established some time after the Titan War. Dwarves hate to write and read and tries to avoid it at all cost. Thus, Dwarvish is mostly only ever spoken, and because of that, is a very hard languague to study or learn if you are not born a dwarf in a dwarven society. It is common to carve names of people or objects in rock or metal as a form of honor, but beyond that, the only time dwarvish is ever written is in letters consisting of not more than a single sentance. Dwarvish has a letter that establishes either a question or statement, and the sentance follows thereafter. It can be noted that letters are only written and sent in cases of emergency, mostly among nobles or kings in times of war.

Ancient Dwarvish

Typical speakers: Linguists & historians.

No one has uttered Ancient Dwarvish since the Titan War. After the war, the dwarves wanted to enter a new age togerther, and leave all the hardships behind, and so, chose to also stop speaking in their common tongue, adapting Common as their new primary languague. Yet with time, a new revised version of the languague slowly crept up.

Ancient Elvish

Typical speakers: None.

Also known as the languague of the Atelya. It has since been lost to time, and none live today that know it. Only a select few words have ever been documented.

Elvish

Typical speakers: Noble Elves, Elves & Half-elves.

The common languague for all kinds of Elves. Noble Elves speak it with a certain accent, considered to be slightly more dignified than the accent used by "normal" Elves and Half-elves.

Halfling

Typical speakers: Halflings.

Halflings have their own languague that is rarely used anymore. Most still learn it as they grow up, but there is very little use for it in practice. Since Halflings often live together with other humans Common is much more practical.

Myao

Typical speakers: Felines, most animals.

Most animals speak Myao, Felines being the only Human Race that know it. It is nearly impossble for other humans to learn it as the concept of animal speech seems like just gibberish to most.

Orcish

Typical speakers: Orcs, Goblins, Hobgoblins.

Orcish is like a scrambled version of Elvish, with a strong accent, making it hard for Elves to understand. Only a very keen eared Elf can make out some of the words and make meaning out of Orcish.

Troll

Typical speakers: Trolls, Giants.

Troll is a languague with few words. There are almost none who speak it beyond Trolls or Giants, making it hard to study for Human Races.

Demon Speech

Typical speakers: Demons, creatures from the Abyss.

Demon Speech is the languague of the Abyss. All demons speak it, including other monsters and terrible creatures the live in or near the Abyss.

Infernal

Typical speakers: Demonkin, abyssal worshippers.

Infernal is the main languague for Demonkin, but is also often spoken by cultists and demon worshippers.

Snake Tongue

Typical speakers: Dragonfolk, snakes, Lizardmen.

Most races with a snake tongue can speak the languague with the same name.

Dragon Tongue

Typical speakers: Dragons.

Dragon Tongue is the first languague that was ever developed in the world of Thorczhia. It was made by the first dragons in three days and the languague has seen some few iterations over the thousands of years it has existed to account for new words and concepts that has come into existance since its inception.

Kobold

Typical speakers: Kobolds.

Kobold is a languague universally understood by Dragons and many Dragonfolk, Lizardmen and snakes. To others it is just as much gibberish as the way animals speak. Is it spoken by making the infamous Kobold noise over and over.

Elemental

Typical speakers: Elementals, Ghosts.

Elemental is a languague that cannot be heard by Human ears. It is spoken through the minds of the elementals or ghosts and only one well versed in magic or necromancy could understand it.

Windtune

Typical speakers: Windfarers.

Windfarers are people who can travel with the wind by tuning their lifeforce to it. These people have also learned to communicate through the "song" of wind itself.

Deep Speech

Typical speakers: Deep sea creatures & creatures far below ground.

To hear the languague of the creatures of the Deep is to hear madness incarnate. Few Human Races can hear its sound without losing their minds.

Celestial

Typical speakers: Gods, Angels.

The Gods of Thorczhia, as well as their protectors, the Angels, have a unique languague spoken exclusively by them. It is literally impossible for a mortal to ever understand its words.

Cosmic

Typical speakers: None

Cosmic is a hypothetical languague made up by scholars. It is the idea of a languague that anyone can understand, and allows you to understand all languagues spoken to you as well. Anyone that achives True Enlightment, supposedly, should be able to speak this languague, according to high profile wizards.
